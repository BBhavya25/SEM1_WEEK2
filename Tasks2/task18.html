The Great Library Escape

Once upon a time, there was a man named Mr. Wiggins, who worked as a librarian in a small town. He loved books, peace, and quiet. In fact, he loved quiet so much that the slightest noise would drive him absolutely crazy. His motto was: “A silent library is a happy library.”
One rainy Tuesday, things were just as they always were: perfectly quiet. Mr. Wiggins was happily organizing books when a group of mischievous teenagers stormed in. They weren't interested in reading; they just wanted to escape the rain. And, well, they brought the storm As soon as they entered, chaos ensued. They were shouting, laughing, and, to Mr. Wiggins' horror, playing music on their phones. He rushed over, with his mustache twitching angrily, and hissed, “This is a library! You must be quiet!” The teenagers, of course, ignored him and continued their ruckus. One of them even climbed a bookshelf to sit on top and pretend he was the "king of the library." Now, Mr. Wiggins had never encountered such rebellious behavior before. Usually, a stern glare was enough to silence even the noisiest visitors. But these kids were relentless. As they got louder and louder, Mr. Wiggins felt his patience slipping away like a bookmark in a windstorm. Then, something remarkable happened. Mr. Wiggins snapped. With the speed of a cheetah and the fury of a school principal, he ran to the fuse box in the corner and turned off all the lights. The library went pitch black, and the teenagers fell silent. "Who's there?" one of them whispered."You," Mr. Wiggins said in a low, dramatic voice, “have disturbed the spirits of the library." "The what?" another asked, now sounding nervous. "The spirits,” Mr. Wiggins said, slowly moving through the dark, his footsteps echoing ominously. "They were once the greatest librarians of all time. They kept this place sacred, silent. And now, they are awake because you have broken the most important rule of the library!" One of the boys started to whimper. "I... I don't want to be haunted by librarian ghosts." "Too late," Mr. Wiggins said in a whispery voice, moving closer. “The spirits will make you silent... forever.” Suddenly, Mr. Wiggins let out a loud, haunting moan—"Shhhhhhhh!"—that echoed through the darkened library. The teenagers screamed, dropped their phones, and ran out of the library as fast as their legs could carry them, knocking over chairs and stumbling over their own feet. Satisfied with his performance, Mr. Wiggins calmly flipped the lights back on and picked up the scattered phones. As the rain cleared outside, the library returned to its usual peaceful state. The following day, Mr. Wiggins found a note taped to the door. It read: “We're sorry. We will never disturb the spirits of the library again. Please don't haunt us. -The Teens”
From that day forward, Mr. Wiggins became a local legend. Rumor had it that the library was haunted by ghostly librarians, and anyone who dared to make too much noise would be shushed into oblivion. The story spread far and wide, and the library became the quietest, most peaceful place in town once again.
Mr. Wiggins smiled to himself every time he passed the fuse box. Little did they know, the only spirit they had to fear was a very grumpy, very creative librarian. And so, the Great Library Escape became the most whispered-about tale in the history of libraries.
